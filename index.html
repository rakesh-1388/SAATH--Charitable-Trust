SAATH

Charitable Trust

Saath is a Public Charitable Trust, founded in 1989. The organization with the vision of creating including urban societies is dedicated to empowering the marginalized communities, by providing livelihood opportunities, access to health care, education, financial literacy and inclusion through varied interventions developed organically in a participatory manner. The goal is to build ownership from within the community to ensure sustainability and impactful transformations. Saath Charitable Trust believes in building inclusive cities where everyone can thrive and works with marginalized communities—especially informal sector workers, migrants, and slum residents while partnering with citizens and other stakeholders to realise this vision.

SMART CITIES

Breaking The Poverty Barrier

This inspiring book by Rajendra Joshi captures the 30-year journey of Saath, a pioneering organization working toward inclusive and rights-based urban development. More than a story of impact, it reflects on the challenges, learnings, and evolution of Saath as a resource and thought leader in the development space. With powerful endorsements from sector leaders, the book offers insights for practitioners, policymakers, and citizens striving to make cities more equitable. It invites readers to reflect, engage, and contribute to meaningful social change.

MEHNAT MANZIL

Celebrating Informal Workers and their contribution to our cities and lives

Saath has set up Mehnat Manzil – a Museum of Work that honours the lives of informal sector workers and sheds light on their everyday realities, including migration, livelihoods, housing, and infrastructure.

Visitors to the museum journey through powerful stories of resilience, community-driven change, and remarkable ingenuity, offering a moving glimpse into the strength and spirit of those often overlooked.
